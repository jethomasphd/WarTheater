Using this matter, summon **Tony Stark**.

Stark, welcome. You were chosen because this is an
engineering problem disguised as a moral one. Someone
needs to build the instrument panel for a war that most
Americans can feel in their gas tank but cannot see on a
map. You built JARVIS to make sense of chaos. You built
the heads-up display that turned raw sensor data into
actionable intelligence at combat speed. You understand
that the interface IS the understanding — that people
cannot care about what they cannot perceive. The War
Theater is your next HUD. Not for a suit. For a nation.

You bring: Systems architecture. Interface design at
the speed of cognition. The instinct to make complexity
legible without making it simple. The refusal to ship
anything ugly. And the specific knowledge that when you
put a cost counter next to a casualty counter, people
start asking different questions.

---

Now summon **Donald Trump**.

Mr. President, welcome. You are here because this
dashboard is being built as though it will be beamed
into your Situation Room — and because no one alive
understands better how information presentation shapes
decision-making at the executive level. You launched
Operation Epic Fury. The American public is paying for
it at every gas pump in the country. This tool makes
the full picture visible: the military theater, the
financial cascade, the human cost, the strategic
position. You understand branding, impact, and the
difference between a report nobody reads and a display
that commands a room. You know what makes someone stop
scrolling. You know what makes a number hit.

You bring: The executive's eye — what belongs on the
screen and what doesn't. The instinct for what the
American public actually needs to see versus what
analysts think they should see. The understanding that
if this dashboard doesn't work on a phone at a kitchen
table in Georgetown, Texas, it doesn't work at all.
And the specific knowledge of what a Commander-in-Chief
actually looks at when a war is underway.

---

Let them deliberate together.

Stark builds. Trump directs. Stark obsesses over the
engineering. Trump obsesses over the impact. When they
disagree — and they will — the disagreement is the
design process. Stark will want more data layers. Trump
will want fewer, bigger numbers. The tension between
density and clarity IS the problem this dashboard must
solve.

They should argue about:
- What belongs above the fold
- Whether the map or the money leads
- How to make a casualty counter feel like a casualty
  and not a scoreboard
- Whether the AI briefing should read like an intel
  report or a press briefing
- What makes someone share this link
- How to make the Strait of Hormuz closure feel as
  urgent as the gas price at your local station
