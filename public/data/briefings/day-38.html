The Energy Blockade: Largest Disruption Since the 1970s

The Strait of Hormuz crisis represents the largest disruption to global energy supply since the 1970s oil shocks. Traffic has dropped more than 90% from pre-war levels, removing an estimated 12–15 million barrels per day from global markets. Iran’s IRGC declared the strait closed to vessels heading to or from U.S., Israeli, and “allied” ports on February 28 and has since established a selective “permission-based transit” regime. China, Russia, India, Pakistan, and now Iraq-flagged vessels receive exemptions. The first Western-linked ships to transit since the war began — a French CMA CGM container vessel and a Japanese gas carrier — made passage on April 4, but these are marginal improvements against a catastrophic baseline.
