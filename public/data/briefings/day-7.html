Humanitarian Crisis Accelerates

The 450,000 displaced in Lebanon represents the fastest displacement since the 2006 Lebanon War. Flows are primarily northward toward Tripoli and eastward toward Syria — ironic given Syria's own ongoing instability. Medical facilities in southern Lebanon operate at or beyond capacity, with at least three hospitals reporting structural damage from Israeli strikes on adjacent Hezbollah positions. The pattern of strikes near civilian infrastructure raises questions about proportionality that will intensify as the war continues.

In Iran, the 400+ civilian casualty figure from state media is likely both politically motivated (to build international sympathy) and understated (given the scale of strikes on dual-use infrastructure in populated areas). The information blackout created by media access restrictions means the true toll may not be known for months or years.

The Interceptor Arithmetic

The Iron Dome interceptor sustainability issue foreshadows a broader problem affecting all parties. Israel has expended 1,500+ interceptors in 7 days. The US has been burning through Patriot and NASAMS interceptors defending Gulf bases. Iran has been expending its ballistic missile and drone stockpile. The question for each side is whether production and logistics can sustain the consumption rate. The IDF officer's warning of "weeks, not months" of supply for the northern front is the first public acknowledgment of a constraint that will increasingly shape military decisions.
