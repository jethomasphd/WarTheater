Three U.S. Carriers in the Middle East — First Time Since 2003 — Meets a Drained Stockpile

The defining military move of Day 56 is the 3-CSG concentration CENTCOM publicly announced via X. The arrival of the USS George H.W. Bush (CVN-77) in the Indian Ocean (after a deliberate Cape Agulhas / Mozambique Channel transit that bypassed Bab el-Mandeb) joined the USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72) in the Northern Arabian Sea and the USS Gerald R. Ford (CVN-78) in the Red Sea. The combined posture — >200 aircraft, 15,000 sailors and Marines, 9+ DDGs, plus the Tripoli ARG — is the first three-carrier Middle East concentration since Operation Iraqi Freedom in 2003. Bush escorts (USS Mason, USS Donald Cook, USS Ross plus DESRON 22 staff and the fast oiler USNS Arctic) arrived intact. This is escalation-dominance posture, telegraphed deliberately the day before Witkoff and Kushner fly to Pakistan. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth said at a Friday press briefing the blockade will continue “as long as it takes.” CENTCOM’s redirect tally rose to 34 vessels turned back since April 13 (SOF News cites 33), up from 31 on April 23.

The counterweight is munitions math. The New York Times reported on Apr 24 that the U.S. has expended more than 1,200 Patriot interceptors ($4M+ each), roughly 1,100 long-range stealth cruise missiles (close to the total remaining stockpile), and over 1,000 Tomahawks — approximately ten years of annual procurement — during Operation Epic Fury.
